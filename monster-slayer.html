The Challenge

Overview & Scope

Monster Slayer is a simple yet challenging 2D platformer game that requires the player to defeat all enemies and bosses to progress through the levels. Built natively in the Unity Engine, the game leverages robust physics controllers to ensure precise, responsive jumps and attacks.

The game's different themes, varied enemy archetypes, and environmental obstacles provide escalating difficulty. A core design philosophy was ensuring that limited pick-ups and distinct attack patterns force players to adopt strategic gameplay decisions rather than relying on brute force to overcome the multi-phase boss fights.

My Role & Contributions

As the sole Game Designer and Programmer on this project, I managed the entire end-to-end development cycle:

  • Kinematic Controllers: Engineered responsive 2D physics controllers for the player to ensure pixel-perfect jumping, dashing, and combat collision frames.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Designed robust Event-Driven State Machines in C# to govern enemy patrolling patterns, aggro ranges, and complex boss stages.
  • Level Architecture: Crafted varied obstacle courses and incorporated a dynamic resource inventory system to balance the learning curve and maintain tension.

Core Technology Stack

  • Unity 2D Engine: Scene management, tilemap painting, animator controllers.
  • C# Scripts: Object-oriented design patterns (Singletons, State Machines, Object Pooling).
  • Physics 2D: Rigidbodies, BoxColliders, Raycasting for grounded/wall interaction detection.
  • Asset Management: Handling sprite sheets, audiosource arrays, and particle effects.

Architecture

Gameplay Systems

Finite State Machines (FSM)

  • Enemy AI Logic: Enemies transition dynamically between Idle, Patrol, Chase, and Attack states based on real-time distance and line-of-sight raycasting to the player transform.
  • Boss Phases: Advanced bosses utilize nested FSMs to radically alter their attack patterns and movement speeds when health drops below critical thresholds.

Object Pooling

  • Projectile Management: Instead of rapidly instantiating and destroying memory-heavy prefabs (arrows, magic blasts), the system utilizing Object Pooling to recycle hidden assets, eradicating Garbage Collection lag spikes on low-end hardware.
